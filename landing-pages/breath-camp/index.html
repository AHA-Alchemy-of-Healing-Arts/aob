What BreathCamp Is

A week of breath,
body, and community.

BreathCamp is a 7-day residential retreat that combines conscious breathing with movement, sound, and the kind of deep connection that only happens when you live and practice alongside others for a full week.

It is led by Anthony Abbagnano and a team of senior Alchemy of Breath trainers, on 15 hectares of quiet Tuscan land. Three meals a day. Plant-based, locally sourced. Practice spaces under the open sky when the weather is kind.

The week is designed for people who want to deepen their personal practice, find their community, and spend time in one of the most beautiful places in Italy. No prior breathwork experience required.

A Needed Pause

When the world feels loud,
stillness becomes a form of care.

The last few years have asked a lot of us. Wars that do not end, headlines that do not soften, a climate that keeps reminding us it is listening, and a pace of life that treats rest like a luxury instead of a requirement. If your nervous system feels tired, it is not a personal failing. It is a reasonable response to the moment we are living in.

A week in Tuscany will not fix what is happening in the world, and we will not pretend otherwise. What it can offer is a quiet room to set things down for a while. Slow mornings, honest breath, food grown nearby, and the company of people who also came looking for ground beneath their feet. Not an escape from your life, but a place to remember how to meet it.

The Journey

Seven days. One natural arc.

Every day has its own focus, but the week moves as one piece. From arrival, through deep practice, to integration and home.

01

Day One

Arrival and meeting

Land at ASHA. Settle in. Meet the people you will share the week with. Evening drum circle as the sun sets over the hills.

02

Day Two

Foundations of breath

The fundamentals of conscious breathing. Beginner and advanced techniques. Support for your own personal process.

03

Day Three

Deepening and celebration

Professional breathwork tools and how to use them. Evening dance celebration under the Tuscan sky.

04

Day Four

The Bridge

Anthony's signature process for moving through what is ready to shift. A day of deep, held work.

05

Day Five

Water and sound

Pool breathwork, sound bath, and evening singing and dancing. A day that moves between the gentle and the joyful.

06

Day Six

Mandala and feast

Anthony's Mandala Breathwork, expert-led sessions, and a closing Italian feast with the community you have built.

07

Day Seven

Integration and home

Morning integration practices. Closing ritual. A shared lunch before you carry the week home with you. The practices you have learned stay with you because you have lived them every day.

A Typical Day

Morning movement. Breakfast. Breathwork or workshop. Group share. Lunch. Breathwork or workshop. Dinner. Free time, or whatever the evening calls for. Spacious, rhythmic, and rooted in the land.

About ASHA

Welcome Home.

ASHA Retreat Centre sits on 15 hectares of untouched Tuscan land near Castell'Azzara. Founded in 2020 by Anthony Abbagnano and Amy Rachelle, it was built as a sanctuary for healing, creativity, and community. A place where all of you is welcome.

Swimming pool, meditation spaces, dining room, group practice rooms including the open-air Prana Shala. Nearby natural hot springs, Etruscan historical sites, and Pitigliano if you want to wander.

Who Comes to BreathCamp

Scientists, doctors, coaches, healers, educators. And people who do not identify as any of those.

BreathCamp draws an international group each session. Some come because they have worked with breath for years. Some come because they have never tried any of this and something in them is asking for it. Both are welcome. The week holds both.
